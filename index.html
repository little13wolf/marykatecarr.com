I’ve loved web design and computerized graphic art since I was a kid. I learned HTML when I was ten and would spend hours designing for fun. I received my bachelor’s degree in Digital Arts and Science, with a minor in Computer Science, from Clarkson University and was fortunate to be able to turn my passion into a career. Now, I am the web developer for St. Lawrence University by day and a freelance developer at night (and on the weekends!).

I believe the web should be beautiful, intuitive and highly functional. I think the project should dictate the choice of technology, not the other way around.

North Country Ministry Donation Database

Drupal

This is a simple Client Managment System designed and implemented in Drupal. Initially, this was designed to only track donors and their donations but was extended through multiple iterations. The system now can track donor and volunteer information, export CSVs to be used in mail-merge mailing lists and generate a variety of reports based on different criteria, including a year-end cumulative donation report.

Optimum Physician Services: Medical Claims Processing Database
MS Access Development

Working closely with OPSC and their client, Saratoga Emergency Physicians P.C., this project was born out of the need for a user-friendly solution to expedite the review of electronic medical records and processing of insurance claims information. Developed in Microsoft Access, this project utilizes user-friendly forms and SQL queries to interact with multiple databases containing protected health information
